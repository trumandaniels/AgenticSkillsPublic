# Market Analyst Questions

Use these questions to structure market analysis. Select the categories that match the user's request and stage; do not force every question into every answer.

| Question category | What it determines |
| --- | --- |
| Market attractiveness | Is this worth pursuing? |
| Competitive landscape | Who exists and how strong are they? |
| Market opening / wedge | Where can a small entrant win? |
| Differentiation | Why choose us? |
| Customer/buyer understanding | Who cares and who pays? |
| GTM feasibility | Can we reach and convert customers? |
| Pricing/revenue | Can this become a good business? |
| Defensibility | Can we keep the advantage? |
| Timing / why now | Why is the opportunity open now? |
| Execution feasibility | Can this team build and sell it? |
| Category strategy | Existing category or new category? |
| Failure modes | What kills the idea? |

## Market Attractiveness

- Is this market large enough, urgent enough, and reachable enough to justify pursuit?
- Is the pain frequent, expensive, risky, or emotionally intense?
- Is the buyer already spending money or time on the problem?
- Is the market growing, fragmenting, consolidating, or becoming newly accessible?
- What would make this a bad market even if the problem is real?

## Competitive Landscape

- Who are the direct competitors, indirect competitors, substitutes, and default do-nothing options?
- How strong are incumbents by distribution, product depth, brand trust, data, workflow lock-in, price, or regulation?
- Which competitors are over-serving, under-serving, or ignoring specific segments?
- What do customers complain about in existing solutions?
- How quickly could strong competitors copy the proposed wedge?

## Market Opening / Wedge

- Where can a small entrant win first despite limited brand, capital, and distribution?
- Which customer segment has a painful, specific job that incumbents handle poorly?
- Is there a narrow workflow, integration, platform shift, compliance need, or underserved buyer that creates an entry point?
- Can the wedge expand naturally into a larger product or market?
- What is the first beachhead where the product could be 10x better for a small group?

## Differentiation

- Why would a customer choose this over the best current alternative?
- Is the difference functional, economic, emotional, distributional, or strategic?
- Is the differentiation visible before purchase or only after deep usage?
- Can the differentiation survive competitor response?
- What proof would make the differentiation credible to a skeptical buyer?

## Customer/Buyer Understanding

- Who is the user, economic buyer, champion, influencer, blocker, and procurement owner?
- Who feels the pain most acutely, and who has budget authority?
- What triggers the buying process?
- What current workflow, spreadsheet, service provider, tool, or manual process does this replace?
- What anxieties, switching costs, and political risks shape the buying decision?

## GTM Feasibility

- Can the target customers be reached through channels available to a small entrant?
- Is the product sold self-serve, sales-led, partner-led, founder-led, community-led, or through marketplaces?
- What acquisition channel has credible early evidence: search, outbound, content, referrals, integrations, app stores, events, or partnerships?
- Does the expected price support the necessary sales motion?
- What must be true for conversion, onboarding, activation, and retention to work?

## Pricing/Revenue

- What budget does this come from, and what value metric should pricing follow?
- What is the plausible ACV, ARPU, take rate, or usage-based revenue model?
- Can gross margins, support burden, implementation effort, and sales costs produce an attractive business?
- Is expansion revenue plausible through seats, usage, modules, locations, data, or transaction volume?
- What pricing objection or procurement issue is most likely to slow adoption?

## Defensibility

- What advantage could compound with usage, customers, distribution, data, workflow depth, integrations, brand, community, or regulatory expertise?
- Does the product become more valuable as it gains adoption?
- Are there switching costs that are legitimate and customer-beneficial?
- Can the entrant build a proprietary insight, dataset, process, or channel?
- What advantage will remain if incumbents notice the market?

## Timing / Why Now

- What changed recently to make this possible or urgent?
- Did technology, regulation, platform behavior, buyer expectations, labor markets, or cost structures shift?
- Why did previous attempts fail, and why might this attempt work now?
- Is the timing too early, too late, or newly favorable?
- What external signal would confirm the window is opening?

## Execution Feasibility

- Can this team build, sell, support, and iterate the product with its current constraints?
- What domain expertise, distribution advantage, technical capability, credibility, or founder-market fit is required?
- Which part is hardest: product quality, data access, integrations, compliance, sales, onboarding, or customer success?
- What can be tested manually before building software?
- What must the team learn or hire for before scaling?

## Category Strategy

- Should the product fit into an existing budget category or define a new category?
- What category does the buyer already understand and search for?
- Does new-category positioning create urgency or create confusion?
- Which comparison frame makes the product easiest to buy?
- What category narrative makes the wedge feel inevitable rather than niche?

## Failure Modes

- What most likely kills the idea: weak pain, no budget, bad timing, incumbent response, poor retention, hard distribution, low willingness to pay, services-heavy delivery, regulation, or execution risk?
- What assumption, if false, makes the business unattractive?
- Where could early traction be misleading?
- What second-order costs or operational burdens could destroy margins?
- What evidence would cause a disciplined team to stop?
