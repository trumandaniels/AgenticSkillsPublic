# Market Pull Probing

Use when:
- the founder has several adjacent ideas
- the goal is to find pull without betting the company on one big build

Decision types:
- idea selection
- pre-PMF experimentation
- finding the strongest workflow to pursue

What must be true:
- experiments can ship cheaply
- the attempts all teach about roughly the same buyer or workflow

Why it works:
- many small adjacent probes surface disproportionate pull faster than one large speculative bet

What to copy:
- ship small experiments in the same arena
- reuse the same audience, workflow, or underlying capability so each probe teaches the next one
- watch for unsolicited usage, repeated requests, and willingness to pay
- double down when signal clusters around one workflow

What not to copy:
- random idea hopping across unrelated markets
- volume for its own sake
- long projects that erase the speed advantage

Failure modes:
- learning nothing cumulative across experiments
- missing obvious pull because the result looks accidental

What to measure:
- signups from relevant buyers
- unsolicited replies or demos
- repeat usage
- conversion or prepayment signal
