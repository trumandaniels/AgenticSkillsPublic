# Free Surface As Distribution

Use when:
- the founder is considering freemium, a free tool, or a limited free plan
- the question is whether free will create more users or only more burden

Decision types:
- freemium
- PLG
- free-tool distribution

What must be true:
- free usage creates distribution, activation, or clear qualification for paid
- marginal cost and support load are manageable

Why it works:
- free is valuable when it keeps a built-in loop alive or demonstrates the paid workflow at the moment of user intent

What to copy:
- keep the free surface narrow and purposeful
- design it to route users toward the paid workflow
- prefer free when product usage naturally spreads to other likely users

What not to copy:
- defaulting to freemium because it sounds modern
- giving away the whole value without a loop

Failure modes:
- free users consuming support without creating demand
- a free tool that is useful but disconnected from paid conversion

What to measure:
- signup to paid conversion from the free surface
- invitation, share, or referral behavior
- support burden per free user
- search demand and repeat usage for free tools
