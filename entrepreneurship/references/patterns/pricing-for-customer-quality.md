# Pricing For Customer Quality

Use when:
- the product seems underpriced
- low-priced customers are support-heavy, impatient, or low-commitment
- the founder is deciding whether a price increase is worth the conversion hit

Decision types:
- pricing
- packaging
- support economics

What must be true:
- the product delivers real value already
- the business can observe support load, retention, or customer quality by plan

Why it works:
- price changes who buys, how they behave, and how expensive they are to support

What to copy:
- test higher pricing on new customers first
- compare revenue per visitor, retention, and support burden instead of conversion alone
- treat pricing as segmentation, not just monetization

What not to copy:
- raising prices with no value story or no trust
- protecting vanity conversion at the expense of customer quality

Failure modes:
- cheap plans attracting the worst-fit users
- underfunding the support quality the product actually requires

What to measure:
- revenue per visitor
- plan-level retention
- support time per customer
- expansion or upgrade behavior
