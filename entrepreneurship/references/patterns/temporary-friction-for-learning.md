# Temporary Friction For Learning

Use when:
- the funnel has traffic but the team still does not understand segment quality or activation blockers
- a short-term conversion hit could buy decisive learning

Decision types:
- onboarding
- activation diagnosis
- segment qualification

What must be true:
- the team has a specific unanswered question
- the friction can be segmented and temporary

Why it works:
- slowing down the right slice of the funnel can produce better insight than keeping a blind fully-automated flow

What to copy:
- route only the uncertain segment into extra questions, demos, or high-touch onboarding
- remove the friction once the learning goal is met

What not to copy:
- adding permanent friction without a learning hypothesis
- slowing the whole funnel because the team lacks focus

Failure modes:
- collecting data nobody uses
- damaging conversion without a clear decision to inform

What to measure:
- quality difference between the high-touch segment and the default flow
- clarity gained on segment fit or activation blockers
- time boxed learning review date
