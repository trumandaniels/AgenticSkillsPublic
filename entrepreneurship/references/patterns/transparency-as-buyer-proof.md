# Transparency As Buyer Proof

Use when:
- the founder wants to share metrics, progress, or build-in-public updates
- the question is whether transparency will help growth or trust

Decision types:
- content strategy
- trust building
- brand positioning

What must be true:
- the shared data proves something the buyer already cares about
- the likely audience overlaps with likely customers

Why it works:
- transparency is persuasive only when it strengthens a buyer-relevant belief

What to copy:
- name the buyer belief first
- share the minimum proof that makes that belief more credible

What not to copy:
- public metrics as performance art for peers
- oversharing because the startup world praises openness

Failure modes:
- attracting attention from non-buyers
- leaking information without strengthening demand

What to measure:
- trust or conversion lift from the transparency asset
- whether the audience engaging with it overlaps with customers
