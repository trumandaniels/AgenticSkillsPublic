# Translate Technical Advantage

Use when:
- the product has a technical differentiator that buyers do not naturally understand
- the founder needs positioning that connects architecture to buyer value

Decision types:
- positioning
- messaging
- technical product marketing

What must be true:
- the technical choice actually prevents or improves a buyer-relevant outcome

Why it works:
- buyers usually care about the consequence of a technical choice more than the mechanism itself

What to copy:
- name the buyer fear or desired outcome first
- keep the technical explanation one layer deeper for the audience that wants it

What not to copy:
- leading with identity language the buyer does not care about
- treating technical elegance as self-evidently valuable

Failure modes:
- vague messaging that sounds impressive but does not map to buyer stakes
- overexplaining technical details before proving relevance

What to measure:
- message comprehension in sales or demo calls
- landing page conversion by message variant
- objection frequency tied to trust, control, or risk
