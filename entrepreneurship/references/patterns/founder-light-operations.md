# Founder-Light Operations

Use when:
- the founder is overloaded
- the business has key-person risk
- the question is about delegation, sellability, or keeping the business calm and durable
- the founder wants the business to tolerate delayed response windows or short absences

Decision types:
- delegation
- hiring
- sellability
- operating design

What must be true:
- meaningful knowledge, process, or customer trust currently lives inside the founder

Why it works:
- reducing dependence improves current operating quality and future optionality at the same time

What to copy:
- document recurring work
- move repetitive support or ops off the founder once good judgment is understood
- ask what breaks if the founder disappears for 30 days
- automate low-judgment support or refund paths before adding management overhead

What not to copy:
- delegating work the founder never learned well enough to manage
- building exit theater before operational quality
- choosing a product shape that requires founder heroics when the goal is a calm business

Failure modes:
- founder bottlenecks hidden behind heroics
- complexity widening diligence and shrinking the buyer pool
- lifestyle mismatch causing burnout before the system is durable

What to measure:
- tasks that still require founder intervention
- support and ops volume handled without the founder
- cleanliness and repeatability of reporting and processes
