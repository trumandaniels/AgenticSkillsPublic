# Founder-Led Trust Surface

Use when:
- the founder is deciding whether to build in public or become the visible face of the business
- trust, education, or relatability materially affect adoption
- a faceless brand is slowing feedback, distribution, or conversion

Decision types:
- founder brand
- build in public
- community-led acquisition
- trust building

What must be true:
- the visible founder or operator can speak credibly to the buyer's real pains
- the audience being built overlaps with likely buyers, referrers, or community nodes
- the public presence can be sustained without derailing core product work

Why it works:
- in early-stage software and niche markets, people often trust and remember a visible human faster than a faceless brand

What to copy:
- make one real person consistently visible
- share educational material, progress, and buyer-relevant stories
- add value inside the target community without demanding immediate return
- treat founder presence, support tone, and public content as part of the product experience

What not to copy:
- performative posting for founder applause
- building an audience of peers who do not buy
- oversharing metrics or personal details that do not strengthen buyer trust

Failure modes:
- attention coming mostly from other founders instead of customers
- founder content becoming a distraction from product and support
- personality outgrowing product credibility

What to measure:
- inbound signups or leads tied to the founder surface
- reply quality and relationship depth from target buyers
- conversion lift when prospects already know the founder story
- trust signals during support and sales conversations
