# Launch Where Buyers Already Are

Use when:
- the founder is deciding how to launch
- the choice is between a broad tech launch and a concentrated buyer launch

Decision types:
- launch planning
- channel choice
- Product Hunt timing

What must be true:
- the team knows where likely buyers already pay attention
- the product is ready enough to convert concentrated traffic into real usage

Why it works:
- concentrated buyer attention usually beats broad attention when the goal is validation or early traction

What to copy:
- launch first in the highest-density buyer channel available
- treat Product Hunt as amplification after proof, not as discovery
- use direct outreach or early cohorts when trust matters

What not to copy:
- expecting launch platforms to create demand from scratch
- launching broadly before onboarding and positioning are ready

Failure modes:
- weak launch numbers leading to the wrong conclusion that the product is dead
- confusing tech audience applause with buyer pull

What to measure:
- activated users by source
- quality of early feedback
- conversion to retained usage, not just signups or upvotes
