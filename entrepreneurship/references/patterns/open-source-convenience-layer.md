# Open Source Convenience Layer

Use when:
- the product is built on open source
- the founder needs a monetization model that fits the project's values

Decision types:
- monetization
- packaging
- open source business model

What must be true:
- the hosted offer is meaningfully easier, safer, or better configured than self-hosting
- the buyer wants the outcome without the operational burden

Why it works:
- many customers do not want to self-host; they want convenience, reliability, defaults, and less operational babysitting

What to copy:
- charge for the easiest and safest way to get the value
- sell convenience, reliability, integrations, and managed scale

What not to copy:
- assuming the main competitor is self-hosting rather than doing nothing
- monetizing in a way that undermines the trust premise of the project

Failure modes:
- hosted value being too weak relative to self-hosting
- messaging the technical mechanism instead of the buyer outcome

What to measure:
- hosted conversion
- churn reasons tied to operational burden
- buyer response to convenience and reliability messaging
