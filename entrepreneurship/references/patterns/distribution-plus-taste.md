# Distribution Plus Taste

Use when:
- the founder already has audience, reputation, or trusted taste in a domain
- the choice is between a business that compounds existing distribution and one that starts a fresh treadmill

Decision types:
- idea selection
- product portfolio shaping
- audience-to-product fit

What must be true:
- the founder already reaches the relevant audience or can do so credibly
- the founder's judgment or taste materially improves the offer

Why it works:
- existing trust lowers distribution cost while taste makes the offer more differentiated and easier to market

What to copy:
- choose offers that become stronger because the founder already has earned attention and judgment in that space
- favor products, media, templates, or education that reinforce the same audience loop

What not to copy:
- picking ideas only because they look large or prestigious
- assuming audience size matters more than audience relevance

Failure modes:
- creating a second business that demands unrelated distribution
- spreading attention across products that do not reinforce each other

What to measure:
- cost and speed of reaching likely buyers
- overlap between current audience and best customers
- repeatability of organic demand from existing channels
