# Audience-First Discovery

Use when:
- the founder has not found a sharp ICP or problem yet
- the founder has community proximity, niche access, or domain credibility
- the main question is who to serve or where to start

Decision types:
- ICP selection
- niche narrowing
- idea discovery
- positioning from real language

What must be true:
- the target users gather somewhere visible
- the founder can participate credibly
- the founder is willing to learn before scaling

Why it works:
- community proximity produces better problem selection, sharper language, and easier early distribution

What to copy:
- join one real community
- observe repeated pains and workaround language
- narrow the niche until referrals, messaging, and support all get easier

What not to copy:
- building a generic founder audience instead of buyer access
- equating followers with trust
- using audience-building to delay product decisions

Failure modes:
- chasing breadth before specificity
- talking to adjacent peers rather than buyers
- collecting anecdotes without identifying repeated painful workflows

What to measure:
- repeated problem frequency
- willingness to talk or buy
- similarity of language across prospects
- activation and retention quality of the first niche users
