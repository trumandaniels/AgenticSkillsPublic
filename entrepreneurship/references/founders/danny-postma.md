# Danny Postma

## Context

- companies: Headlime, HeadshotPro, ProfilePicture.ai, and several smaller AI products
- edge: fast AI product shipping, niche SEO capture, and aggressive automation
- recurring lesson: choose business shapes that can survive without constant real-time founder presence

## Signature Moves

- ship many adjacent products quickly and let search or public response reveal the strongest pull
- use playful side projects as marketing surfaces for the core product
- let larger competitors expand category demand, then capture narrower intent
- automate refunds, support, and ops before defaulting to more headcount

## Use This Founder When

- the founder wants a calm, support-light portfolio instead of a high-touch startup
- the business has category tailwinds from larger competitors and can win on niche intent or speed
- the founder is deciding whether to sell, automate, or add a very small team

## Do Not Cargo-Cult

- do not chase AI or novelty without real search intent or conversion
- do not build always-on critical tools if you do not want a pager business
- do not hire a team just to preserve growth theater
