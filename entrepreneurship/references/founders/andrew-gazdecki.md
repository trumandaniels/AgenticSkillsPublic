# Andrew Gazdecki

## Context

- company: Acquire, formerly MicroAcquire, a startup acquisition marketplace
- edge: seller empathy, stair-step thinking, and founder-light systems
- recurring lesson: sellability is usually downstream of operational quality, not exit theater

## Signature Moves

- favor the side of the market that incumbents underserve
- build trust, privacy, and education into sensitive workflows
- choose business models the founder can realistically win now
- reduce founder dependence and operational mess early

## Use This Founder When

- the founder is deciding how ambitious or operationally heavy the next business should be
- the business is founder-heavy and thinking about delegation or sellability
- trust and privacy are gating adoption in a sensitive workflow

## Do Not Cargo-Cult

- do not jump into a complex model just because it feels exciting
- do not optimize for exit theater without a good business underneath it
- do not delegate functions the founder never understood well
